    FILLING CONTAINERS

    • The weight of the cargo must be evenly distributed over the largest possible floor area.
    • The center of gravity of the cargo should be as close as possible to the center of the container and as low as possible. The higher the center of gravity, the higher the wedging devices must be.
    • Cargo load units must support each other (to be in contact) with no space between and must be secured to the container (holds, lashings, etc.) so that they can not move or collapse. the containers are equipped with several tie-down rings and bars.
    • If the loading units are not homogeneous, the heavier ones must be on the ground and the lighter ones at the top (and the liquids below the solids).
    • The maximum gross weight of the container (usually marked "Gross Max" on the right side door of the container) should never be exceeded (there are also maximum limits for different local land transport regulations). It is illegal for the weight of the cargo to exceed the net weight (payload) value indicated on the same right side door.
